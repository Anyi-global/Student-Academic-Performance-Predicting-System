About the system

What Student Academic Risk Prediction System is designed to do

Student Academic Risk Prediction System is a school support tool that helps staff notice early signs of academic struggle, review them in one place, and respond before the student falls further behind.

Purpose Support early intervention

It helps schools spot students who may need help sooner, while there is still time to respond effectively.

Users Teachers, counselors, administrators

It is built for staff who already make decisions about tutoring, follow-up, and student support planning.

Role Decision support, not replacement

It adds structure to conversations, but final judgment should still come from school staff.

Who should use it

Best fit for schools that want a clearer support workflow

Useful for

  • Teachers reviewing a student whose attendance or work quality is slipping
  • Counselors preparing for intervention meetings or student check-ins
  • School leaders who want a more consistent way to discuss student support

Less useful for

  • Making final decisions without staff review
  • Predicting long-term life outcomes or personal behavior
  • Replacing direct knowledge from teachers, families, or student records
What goes into an assessment

The tool looks across several parts of a student profile

Personal and family context

Basic student details, family structure, and home-related background information.

Attendance and previous setbacks

Absences and earlier failures, which are often strong warning signs when reviewed together.

Study habits and school support

Study time, family support, school support, and whether extra help may already be in place.

Math and Portuguese course patterns

The prototype uses details from both subjects to form a fuller picture of the student situation.

How to read the result

The score should guide response, not replace judgment

At Risk

The student shows a pattern similar to others who needed support. This should trigger closer review and a practical follow-up plan.

Not At Risk

The student currently looks more stable, but staff should still keep protective routines in place and monitor for change.

0% to 39%

Routine monitoring. Continue normal follow-up and recheck if attendance, study habits, or engagement change.

40% to 69%

Targeted support. Review missed work, weak subjects, and attendance before the concern becomes more serious.

70% and above

Urgent support. Move quickly with family contact, tutoring, and short-cycle review.

About the underlying model

What powers the prototype

The current prototype uses a decision-tree model trained on the UCI Student Performance Dataset. The dataset combines records from Portuguese secondary school students in Mathematics and Portuguese.

  • 49 input features are used in the current prototype
  • The result is a two-class outcome: At Risk or Not At Risk
  • The percentage is meant to support prioritization and follow-up planning
Important safeguards

What this system should never be used for

Do not use it as

  • A final judgment on a student's ability or future
  • A replacement for teacher feedback or direct student support
  • A standalone reason for punishment, exclusion, or labeling

Use it instead as

  • An early warning signal that supports staff discussion
  • A structured way to prioritize cases and document follow-up
  • A starting point for conversation with the student, family, and support team
Important: Student Academic Risk Prediction System is a decision-support aid only. Each prediction should be reviewed alongside teacher judgment, recent student performance, and the student's wider school and personal context.
